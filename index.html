WiDS Datathon workshop

Datathon workshop is a chance for individuals with an interest in data science to come together, learn and improve their skills in a supportive and enjoyable setting. It is open to people of all levels of expertise, from beginners to experts, and offers something valuable to everyone who participates.

The workshop is targeted at beginners with no prior programming experience or students in non-STEM fields. If you are a math or engineering student, you likely will have too much experience for this workshop to benefit you.

What is the format of the workshop?

We will help participants to install the software and demonstrate how to run it to write simple Python commands.

Club members are helping to co-organize the event, teach curriculum and provide one-on-one assistance to participants.

What am I going to learn?

The workshop will cover different programming languages curriculum designed to give beginners complete, basic programming skills to use in their studies or workplace. The curriculum will cover basic programming concepts such as variables, data-types, branching, loops, functions and usage of libraries. Over the course of the workshop, participants will learn the Python programming language through lectures and hands-on sessions, supported by a team of programming club at SSU.
